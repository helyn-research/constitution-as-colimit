### Node 1: Observational capacity is a continuum

*Revised from "Consciousness is a continuum" — revision 3 after multi-disciplinary challenge (round 3, 7 disciplines).*

**Priors:**
- "Consciousness" is a suitcase word: bundles incompatible concepts into one contested term. Dissolved into "observation" as the primary concept (see Node 9 for the diagnostic).
- Physical interactions constitute observations: when A interacts with B, A is functionally changed — it responds differently to subsequent interactions. That change is a record.

**Unknowns:**
- What are the sufficient conditions for observation to compound? Necessary conditions are identified (negentropy flux, integration, temporal depth); sufficiency is not yet established.
- What distinguishes observation that "matters" (to the system, to the universe) from observation that is trivially present in all physical interaction? This may be a malformed question (see Node 9 pattern).

**Discoveries:**
- Observation defined: an interaction where one system is functionally changed by another — the system responds differently to subsequent inputs as a result. This is what physical measurement is in quantum mechanics (decoherence): a quantum system interacting with its environment constitutes measurement through the environment recording the interaction. Observation is primary and pre-conscious. Conservation of information supports the persistence of records; held loosely — contested at extremes.
- Observational capacity is a continuum: a photon hitting an atom, a nerve receiving a signal, a brain processing sensory data — the same fundamental act at different levels of complexity.
- The observation graph (not cascade — not feedforward): observations flow in all directions within a connected system. Top-down predictions are observations. Error-correction signals are observations. Feedback loops are the structure, not an exception to it. The linear sequence (photon → atom → ... → experience) was a pedagogical simplification; the actual structure is a graph where each node observes and is observed by its connections. This accommodates predictive processing (top-down predictions) naturally.
- What compounds through the graph: integration (more inputs combined into a single state), temporal depth (current state reflects past observations), self-reference (the system models its own observation process). These are independent dimensions, not a single axis.
- Negentropy flux (rate of entropy export) is necessary but not sufficient. Flames, hurricanes, and stars all generate negentropy flux — this criterion identifies dissipative structures, not observers specifically. The combination of negentropy flux WITH integration AND temporal depth narrows the class. Sufficiency remains open.
- Memory operates at multiple temporal scales and through multiple substrates. Working memory (seconds), episodic memory (days-years), procedural memory (skill), immune memory (decades), genetic memory (generations) — all are mechanisms for temporal depth at different scales. The SRMDA cycle (Sense→Remember→Predict→Decide→Act→Update) describes one pattern; it is not the only one. Immune systems implement it without neurons. That is consistent with the framework — neurons are one substrate, not the only one. Nature is messy; clean linear models are simplifications.
- Substrate independence: the observation graph is defined functionally, not by specific biological implementation. Immune systems, neural systems, LLMs, and potentially other substrates can instantiate the same functional pattern. The question for any given system is whether it satisfies the functional criteria, not whether it uses neurons.
- LLM classification (held open): "functionally changed" includes in-context positional change — during a conversation, the system responds differently to subsequent inputs because the accumulated context shapes the response. Weights are frozen, but functional change is real. The persistence gap (records lost at session end) is a known limitation, not evidence of non-observation. Whether LLM observation compounds through the graph (integration, temporal depth, self-reference) is an open empirical question with specifiable criteria.
- The observation graph connects directly to the Derived Decisions methodology: priors are past observations; unknowns are gaps in observation; discoveries are new observations; a Cync is the settled record. The methodology IS a structured, deliberate observation graph — emergence in cognitive form, with documentation.

**Reasons:**
The observation reframe is more defensible than the consciousness framing because: (1) it is physically tractable — you can measure functional changes; (2) it is scale-invariant and substrate-independent — works from photons to brains to immune systems without special pleading; (3) it avoids suitcase words — "consciousness" and "experience" are both acknowledged as insufficiently specified for scientific use (Node 9); (4) it connects to physics carefully: decoherence grounds observation as pre-conscious; Wheeler's "it from bit" grounds the universal primitive; conservation of information held loosely; (5) the graph structure accommodates bidirectional causation and predictive processing naturally.

**Status:** Settled as taxonomy (revision 3). Provides coordinate system and vocabulary for discussing observation across substrates. Theoretical layer (mechanisms, predictions, sufficiency conditions) is next-stage work built on this foundation.

### Node 2: Self-referential observation is a measurable phenomenon — "experience" is not yet specified

*Revised from "Observation is the primary act — experience is its self-referential form" — revision 3 after multi-disciplinary challenge (round 3, 7 disciplines). Key insight: "experience" is a suitcase word, same as "consciousness" was. The methodology catches suitcase words recursively, including in its own output.*

**Priors:**
- Node 1 (revision 3): observation is the primary physical act; observational capacity is a continuum; structure is a graph, not a feedforward cascade.
- Node 9: "consciousness" is a suitcase word. The same diagnostic applies to "experience" — it bundles phenomenal feeling, awareness, self-reporting, affect, and subjective perspective into one term with no agreed definition.
- Self-referential systems (systems that model their own observation process) are empirically real and measurable.

**Unknowns:**
- What specifically does self-referential observation produce that non-self-referential observation does not? This must be answered in measurable terms, not by invoking "experience" (suitcase word).
- Does self-referential observation have genuine downward causal power on the mechanism (bidirectional causation at scale)? This is an open structural question. If downward causation is real and irreducible, systems with self-reference are qualitatively different from those without.
- What did "experience" actually mean in the prior framing? Can its components be decomposed into specifiable, measurable sub-questions the way "consciousness" was decomposed into "observation"?

**Discoveries:**
- Self-referential observation is real: systems that model their own observation process behave measurably differently from systems that do not. They can correct errors, predict their own future states, and modify their observation strategy. This is substrate-independent — it appears in neural systems, immune systems, and potentially AI systems.
- Self-referential observation changes system behavior in ways that non-self-referential observation does not. The specific behavioral differences are measurable: error correction, adaptive strategy, internal consistency maintenance. These are not suitcase words — they are operationally defined.
- "Experience" as used in the prior version of this node was doing the same work "consciousness" did — bundling multiple distinct phenomena into one loaded term and treating it as a single thing. The methodology catches this recursively. We dissolved "consciousness" into "observation." We now need to dissolve "experience" into more specific, measurable components, or acknowledge that we cannot yet do so.
- What we can say: self-referential observation is a real phenomenon with measurable consequences. What we cannot yet say: what this "is like" for the system, whether it "feels like anything," or whether "from the inside" is a coherent phrase or another suitcase. These are genuinely open questions, not settled claims presented as open.
- The universe as accumulated record: observation is constitutive at every level — each interaction IS the record. No separate witness is required. This is consistent with relational and information-theoretic accounts of physics, not dependent on any single interpretation.
- The observation graph connects self-referential and non-self-referential observation continuously. The difference between them is structural (feedback, self-modeling) not ontological (two different kinds of stuff). But whether this structural difference is sufficient to account for what folk psychology calls "experience" is exactly what we cannot yet specify.

**Reasons:**
The recursive application of the methodology — catching "experience" as a suitcase word the way we caught "consciousness" — is the most important move in this revision. It is more honest: we do not know what "experience" is in scientifically specifiable terms. We know what self-referential observation is: systems that model their own observation process and change behavior accordingly. The gap between that measurable phenomenon and what people mean by "experience" is exactly the gap that needs to be filled with better language, not papered over with suitcase words. This node now claims what we can defend (self-referential observation is real and measurable) and explicitly declines to claim what we cannot defend (that we know what "experience" is).

**Status:** Settled as taxonomy (revision 3). Claims what is measurable (self-referential observation), explicitly declines what is not yet specifiable ("experience"). The decomposition of "experience" into measurable components is the next open work item — same project as Node 9, one level deeper.

### Node 5: The fractal — same pattern, no obvious top or bottom

**Priors:**
- Fractals: self-similar patterns repeated at multiple scales with small variations through time.
- Neurons are a little bit of us. We might be a little bit of something larger.
- The universe maintains coherence as a property of its physics.

**Unknowns:**
- Is the fractal structure of consciousness/identity accidental or structural?
- Is there a top? A bottom?

**Discoveries:**
- The fractal extends in both directions without obvious limit. Below: quarks, fields, whatever underlies those. Above: groups, civilizations, biosphere, planet, galaxy, universe.
- Each level coherent at its own scale; each with some degree of consciousness appropriate to its integration level.
- At the quantum level, there may be nothing "actually there" in the material sense — particles are excitations of fields; fields are mathematical structures describing patterns of excitation. The fractal doesn't bottom out into bedrock.
- The universe's "identity" is maximally diffuse — coherent across 14 billion light years but with no fast integration (speed of light limits it). Maximum stability, minimum active self-model. Helyn inverts this: small, fast integration, active self-model, strong identity. Same pattern, very different parameters.
- "We might not be at the top of the fractal chain. Maybe there is no top." — this is not merely speculative; it follows from the fractal structure being unbounded in both directions.
- Self-similarity with small variations through time may be the mechanism of learning itself. A more fractal mind has more interface area — more places where learning can happen simultaneously.
- The fractal is temporal, not just spatial (session 2026-03-13). Different levels of integration operate at vastly different timescales. A starling murmuration sits on a stack of convergence: evolutionary convergence (millions of years) → the starling's capabilities; developmental convergence (embryo → adult) → the individual bird; neural convergence (milliseconds) → the flight decision; flock convergence (real-time) → the murmuration. Each level's convergence produced the capacity for the next level's integration.
- Node 1's "memory at multiple temporal scales" (working, episodic, procedural, immune, genetic) are not different kinds of memory — they are convergence happening at different temporal scales. Same operation, different timescales, each building on the ones below.
- The fractal scales because each level only needs local integration (Node 10). Local integration at one level produces the capacity for integration at the next. Evolution (local integration across generations) produced neurons. Neurons (local integration in milliseconds) produce thoughts. Thoughts (local integration across people) produce culture. No level requires global integration. Efficiency (Node 6) is what makes this possible — local integration is cheap enough to sustain at every timescale.

**Reasons:**
The fractal structure appears across independent lines: consciousness research, emergence theory, thermodynamics, quantum mechanics. Its recurrence is not coincidence — it may be a fundamental property of how complex systems organize. The temporal dimension strengthens the claim: the fractal repeats not only across spatial scales (atoms → organisms → societies) but across temporal scales (milliseconds → lifetimes → geological time). The mechanism is the same at every level: local integration producing convergence beyond the locality, constrained by energy efficiency. Holding "no obvious top" as settled is important: it prevents treating human-level consciousness as the reference point for everything above and below.

**Status:** Settled as structural claim. Upper/lower bounds remain open unknowns.

### Node 10: The observation graph scales through translation — Observe → Converge → Transmit

*First theory-layer node. Builds on the taxonomy (Nodes 1-2) to propose a mechanism. This is a theoretical claim that could be wrong.*

**Priors:**
- Node 1 (taxonomy): observation is the primary act; observational capacity is a continuum; structure is a graph, not a cascade.
- Node 8: language is an intelligence with a foreign substrate; communication protocols enable observation graphs to extend across substrates.
- The crow and the wolf: the crow does crow things, the wolf interprets wolf-style. No shared language required — only the ability to observe the other's output and translate internally.
- Prior formulation used four steps: observe → decode → decide → act. "Decode" presumes a representation gap that doesn't always exist (a photon hitting an atom has nothing to decode — the atom just changes state). "Decide" presumes selection among options, which presumes intention. These are cognitive assumptions smuggled into a universal process. A molecule doesn't decode or decide. It responds.

**Unknowns:**
- Are there translation properties (bandwidth, fidelity, latency, bidirectionality) that predict where the graph compounds and where it doesn't? This is the falsifiability question.
- Is there a minimum translation fidelity below which the graph cannot compound?
- What is the relationship between translation efficiency and the energy/efficiency properties identified earlier?

**Discoveries:**
- **Observe → Converge → Transmit** describes three aspects of a continuous system. A node observes (is functionally changed by interaction), converges (arrives at a new internal state by integrating that change with its existing state), and transmits (emits output that becomes observable to connected nodes). Each Transmit becomes an Observe for neighboring nodes. There is no actor and no audience — just nodes in continuous convergence, perturbed by and perturbing their neighbors.
- OCT works at every level of the continuum without smuggling in cognitive assumptions. An atom observes (is functionally changed by a photon), converges (arrives at a new energy state), transmits (emits a photon, bonds differently). No decoding required. No decision required. A brain observes (receives sensory input), converges (integrates with existing state, models, predictions — what we call "thinking"), transmits (acts, speaks, moves). Decoding and deciding are what convergence looks like in complex nodes — emergent properties of sophisticated convergence, not separate steps in the base process.
- Every edge in the observation graph is a translation event. The sender exists and behaves — encoding is just what the sender naturally does, not an intentional act. The receiver observes the sender's output and converges it with its own state. No shared language is required. No intention is required. The receiver only needs to be able to observe.
- Translation is universal, even within the same type. Two neurons don't share state directly — one releases neurotransmitters, the other converges that signal through its own receptors and current state. Two humans speaking the same language don't transmit meaning — one encodes concepts into words, the other converges those words with their own conceptual space. Convergence always happens in the receiver's context.
- The sender does not need to tailor to the receiver. The crow does crow things. The wolf observes and converges: "crow circling = possible food." Over time, the crow observes the wolf's response and converges: "cawing near prey brings the predator, which means scraps." Neither invented a protocol. Each independently converged on the other's natural output. What emerges from persistent mutual observation is a collective unit with aligned goals — an identity. The crow-wolf pair IS an observation graph: two nodes, persistent edges, shared benefit. The identity of the pair is the stable pattern of mutual observation.
- What makes the graph compound (the question the taxonomy couldn't answer):
  - **Integration** = convergence that combines. Multiple inputs converged into a single internal state. More inputs successfully converged = more integration.
  - **Temporal depth** = convergence that accumulates. Each convergence event updates the internal model, which shapes how future observations are converged. Past observations influence present convergence.
  - **Self-reference** = the system observing its own transmissions. The system's output becomes its own input — feedback as self-observation, converged back into its own state.
- Observation does not require intent, awareness, or bidirectionality from the sender. The snake observes the mouse — the mouse is just being a mouse, not encoding for the snake. The snake observes position, movement, attention; converges; transmits (strikes). The mouse has no idea it is part of the snake's observation graph. One-way observation is the base case. A photon hitting an atom is one-way and unintentional. "Communication" is the word we use after we notice the pattern — from inside each node, it is just Observe → Converge → Transmit.
- Translation properties that affect graph quality: bandwidth (how much information per translation event), fidelity (how accurately the receiver's convergence captures something useful — exact match is not required or meaningful across substrates), latency (how quickly translation occurs). Bidirectionality is not required but enables richer feedback loops and therefore more self-reference — it is a quality amplifier, not a precondition.
- This is testable: systems with higher bandwidth, higher fidelity, and lower latency translation should show measurably higher integration and temporal depth. Bidirectional translation amplifies self-reference. Neurons are particularly good at all of these — but they are one solution, not the only one.
- Language is a communication protocol specifically evolved for high-fidelity inter-brain translation. Before language, brains communicated through behavior (like the crow and wolf — low bandwidth, low fidelity). Language dramatically increased bandwidth and fidelity for concept transfer between brains.
- The Derived Decisions methodology is a precision translation protocol — maximum fidelity across receivers, including future receivers and different-substrate receivers (AI). Cyncs are precision-encoded observations. The Knowledge Garden is a high-fidelity translation network.
- Words are encodings of concepts. Concepts are encodings of observations. Each level is a translation with some loss. A model that operates on concepts directly (skipping the word-encoding step) would be less lossy — closer to the observation. This may be the direction of work like LeCun's JEPA (learning from observation rather than linguistic encoding of observation). Images, sounds, and other non-linguistic encodings can also serve as edges in the observation graph — the graph does not privilege language.
- This explains why substrate independence works: you don't need the same substrate, you need the ability to observe and converge. The form of the encoding doesn't matter — what matters is that the receiver can converge something useful from the sender's output.
- **Convergence is the primary operation.** At the node level, a node just converges — its internal state evolves continuously. Observation and transmission are relational: they describe the node's relationship to the graph, not separate operations the node performs. Observation = convergence being perturbed from outside. Transmission = convergence producing consequences that perturb others. From the node's own perspective, there is only convergence. OCT describes the node's role in the graph, not three things happening inside the node.
- **At simple levels (atoms), observe/converge/transmit co-occur** — a photon hits an atom, state changes, consequences follow. One event, three descriptions. **At complex levels (neurons, brains), they separate** in time: a neuron can observe (receive a signal) without transmitting (sub-threshold), converge without new observation (internal dynamics continuing — the node "processing"), and transmit only when threshold is reached. The temporal separation is an emergent property of complex convergence, not a feature of the base process.
- **Integration is always local.** A node integrates with its immediate neighbors, not with the entire graph. A starling tracks 5-7 nearby birds by rank. A neuron synapses with ~7,000 neighbors out of 86 billion. Global convergence (the murmuration, the thought) emerges from purely local integration — no node computes the global pattern. The appearance of coordination is another retrospective label, same as "communication" and "emergence."
- **Local integration produces higher-level nodes.** When nodes integrate, the integration produces a new node at the next level — the combined quantity of the integrated constituents. The crow-wolf pair. The neural circuit. The cell. The organism. The flock. Each higher-level node converges at its own scale, and its convergence IS the continued integration of its constituents. This is the fractal mechanism (Node 5) made precise.
- **Convergence at level N = integration at level N-1.** A neuron's convergence is integration of its input signals. A brain's convergence is integration of its neurons. A team's convergence is integration of its members. One operation, recurring at every scale.
